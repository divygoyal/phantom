# Worked examples — the rubrics applied

Six examples showing the brain in action: from raw source material → Phase 0 intake → Phase 1 rubric pass → draft outline. Each is annotated with *why* not just *what*. Use these as calibration anchors.

## Example 1 — Sora 2 launch (sincere-awe lane)

**Source material:** OpenAI tweet announcing Sora 2 with cameo features. 4 demo clips: a cinematic landscape shot, a cooking process, an absurdist Mario clip, a public-figure ballet (Sam Altman cameo, Sora-2 native).

### Phase 0 intake (reasoning, not just outputs)

**topic_class** = `model-launch` — clear: a new model, not a feature on an existing one.

**emotional_thesis** — what does the viewer leave feeling? Looking at the demo clips: the lipsync is uncanny, the physics in the cooking clip looks correct, the Sam Altman ballet is the absurd flex that proves cameo works. The feeling is "amazement that this is now possible." The viewer rewatches because the brain refuses to accept it on first pass. Going with: **"amazement that this is now possible"**.

**share_trigger** = `awe`. Viewer sends to a friend with "look at this." Not utility (no recipe to use), not argument-bait (no take to argue), not ick (the demos aren't dystopic). It's awe — the visual delta IS the share.

**stakes** = `medium`. The release affects an industry (video creators) but no individual is named as harmed. Not grave.

**hype_charge** = +2 awe. The channel posture is "we genuinely think this is impressive." The visual delta backs the posture — Sam Altman ballet is real cinematic AI now possible. Sincere-awe palette is on the table.

**audience_persona_lean** = `mixed-default`. Topic is global; no India-anchor. Default English caption stack.

**reel_intent** = `launch`. Yes, it's news, but the reel structure should respect the visual delta — V46 12-beat with the launch frame.

**pacing_target** = `40-55s sweet-spot`. Long enough to show 3-4 demo clips; not so long it pads. Going with 45s.

**tonal_target_primary** = sincere-awe. **tonal_target_secondary** = dark-humor at close. The "you have 90 days before this is normal" undercut. Why: the audience is sceptical-by-trade (AI-builder lean strong), and pure sincere-awe risks fanboy-aura. The dark close is the pressure relief — viewer leaves both impressed and slightly unsettled.

**frame_zero_promise** = "100% AI. None of these are real." (5 words). Specific claim. Forces the reel to deliver 4 distinct demo clips that read as not-AI on first watch.

### Phase 1 rubric outputs

**R1 HOOK**: clause 2 fires (reel_intent=launch ∧ has_visual_payoff ∧ hype_charge≥+1). Archetype #1 Product Showcase + AA.2 AI-image-hero (#11-20). But: novelty_budget shows arch #1 used in 2 of last 5 reels. Not yet 3-in-row, so OK to use. Drafting hook: "OpenAI just dropped this. Watch closely — none of this footage is real."

**R2 STRUCTURE**: clause 1 fires (single launch event with visual payoff = V46-style brand-launch). 12-beat, 45s pacing, deviation budget 0.

**R3 TONE**: sincere-awe primary, dark-humor secondary at close.

**R4 EFFECTS**: sincere-awe palette → 2.5D parallax + duotone flash + slow dolly-zoom. Forbidden: glitch RGB, comic-pow, ransom-note (wrong register for awe). Cool-down check: parallax was used 2 reels ago — borderline; let's use it but log as "borderline novelty."

**R5 SFX**: cinematic riser at -22 LUFS into hook reveal; bass-swell + reversed bell on each demo reveal; NO whoosh-on-cut (would kill the silence between cuts that sells the awe). Music bed: light cinematic synth pad at -18 LUFS, evolving — drops to room-tone for the "you have 90 days" close.

**R6 IMAGE STYLE**: cinematic-photoreal-shallow-DOF. Anamorphic 2.39:1, teal-orange grade, halation bloom, Kodak Vision3 500T grain. Style anchor prefix on every Gemini still.

**R7 MOTION VERB**: SUBTLE tier (matches sincere-awe). Verbs: drifts, glints, settles. ONE camera move per Veo clip (5% slow dolly-in). For Sam Altman ballet: animate background/lighting only; do not animate the face beyond a single blink (Veo refusal risk).

### Phase 2 draft skeleton

| Beat | Voice | Visual | SFX |
|---|---|---|---|
| B1 (3s) | "OpenAI just dropped this. Watch closely." | landscape demo, slow dolly-in | cinematic riser starts |
| B2 (4s) | "None of these clips are real footage." | cooking process clip + small caption | bass-swell + reversed bell |
| B3 (5s) | "Watch the lipsync." | Sam Altman ballet, animate BG only | minimal — single ambient pad |
| B4 (5s) | "Look at the physics." | molten-glass-blowing clip | glints + soft pad |
| B5 (4s) | "It's called Sora 2." | logo reveal in cinematic frame | cinematic riser apex |
| B6-B9 (4×3s) | "Cinema. Comedy. Sports. Ads." | 4 distinct genre demos | one diegetic per cut |
| B10 (3s) | "Eight seconds. Seventy-five cents." | price overlay | sub-bass impact |
| B11 (3s) | "[long pause] You have 90 days before this is normal." | tonal shift to dark close, frame fades to black | room-tone bed only |
| B12 (3s) | "Save this for the next time someone says AI peaked." | save-CTA, @aisimplified handle | save-icon ding once |

### Phase 3 critique pass (anticipated)

Score predicted: all dims 4-5. Possible flags:
- Dim 9 novelty: arch #1 used 3rd time in last 5? Borderline; could step down to #4 Specific-Number ("75 cents per second of cinematic AI") if novelty cool-down triggers.
- Dim 6 tonal: secondary tone well-pattern (sincere → dark close — accepted in tonal-palettes.md). Confirm.

If critique forces stepping down to arch #4: rewrite hook as "Eight seconds of cinematic AI for 75 cents. OpenAI just dropped Sora 2." Same structure, different opener.

## Example 2 — Anthropic acquisition rumor (urgent-breaking lane)

**Source material:** Tweet at 9am with screenshot of an internal Slack leak suggesting a $10B offer to Anthropic. Source unnamed; speculation, not confirmed.

### Phase 0 intake

**topic_class** = `acquisition`. Clear from the source.

**emotional_thesis** = "uneasy curiosity about what changes." Not awe (it's news, not a demo). Not ick (no individual harmed yet). Curiosity + unease — the viewer doesn't know yet what this means for them, and wants the channel to tell them.

**share_trigger** = `unease`. Viewer DM-sends to a friend asking "did you see this?". Or saves "for later" to revisit.

**stakes** = `high`. Affects the AI industry; affects individuals using Claude; affects valuation conversations. But no individual harmed yet, so not grave.

**hype_charge** = 0 neutral. Channel is reporting, not celebrating. The audience doesn't want hype on this — they want to understand what changed.

**reel_intent** = `news`. Time-sensitive; channel is one of first voices.

**pacing_target** = `<25s sprint` or `25-40s standard`. Going with 28s — 9-beat news-explainer.

**tonal_target_primary** = urgent-breaking. **secondary** = quiet-observation at close (the "breath beat" at -3s).

**frame_zero_promise** = "Anthropic just got a $10B offer." (6 words.) Specific claim, real number, named protagonist.

### Phase 1 rubric outputs

**R1 HOOK**: clause 1 fires (reel_intent=news ∧ pacing<30s). Archetype #7 Breaking-News + AA.16 wire-photo-from-fiction (#116-120). The wire-photo hook lands here precisely because the rumor isn't confirmed — AP-wire grain + chyron text gives the 600ms photo-trust window.

**R2 STRUCTURE**: clause 3 fires (breaking news, single-fact reveal, pacing<30s). 9-beat news-explainer.

**R3 TONE**: urgent-breaking. EL tags `[urgent][serious]`. Caption stack Anton or Archivo Black caps.

**R4 EFFECTS**: whip-pan + glitch RGB + news-ticker + chyron text. 4 effects (pacing≥45s budget? no, pacing is 28s — drop to 3 effects: whip-pan + news-ticker + chyron).

**R5 SFX**: short riser (1.5s) into hook impact; sub-impact on B6 number reveal; ticker-tone bed at -25 LUFS continuous; one camera-flash burst at AA.16 wire-photo motion start.

**R6 IMAGE STYLE**: AP-wire grain (AA.16 vocabulary). Wire-photo template EE.1 #9. Real-source watermark "Reuters / 2026" pinned bottom-right corner.

**R7 MOTION VERB**: MID tier (news with strong visual). Verbs: subject leans forward 3°, single camera-flash burst at f=20, paper edge curls 2px. NO camera move beyond 5% dolly. 4-second Veo clamp.

### Phase 3 critique pass (anticipated)

Possible flags:
- Source is unconfirmed — dim 1 (hook archetype fit) requires the hook to acknowledge the rumor status. "Anthropic just got a $10B offer" reads confirmed; revise to "Anthropic just got an unconfirmed $10B offer" or "The $10B Anthropic rumor — what we know." Tradeoff: confirmed-sounding hook = stronger pull but credibility-risk if rumor falls through. Channel posture decides.
- The save-CTA is correct (urgent-breaking + unease share_trigger).
- No cha-ching anywhere (correct — guardrail #1 enforced; even at $10B the celebratory bank is forbidden in this register).

## Example 3 — Adobe ships AI design tool (dark-humor lane)

**Source material:** Adobe announcement of a new AI tool that does "what your designer charges $400 for, in 12 seconds." Demo screenshots show a logo in a dropdown, a brand-board generated in one click.

### Phase 0 intake

**topic_class** = `feature-drop`. Existing tool (Adobe) added a feature.

**emotional_thesis** = "uneasy laughter at how fast jobs are folding." Specific. The viewer leaves slightly unsettled — they've seen this happen to writers, now designers. The feeling is recognition.

**share_trigger** = `ick`. "Look how dystopic this is." Viewer DMs the reel to their designer friend.

**stakes** = `high`. Designers as a category are affected. No named individual victim, but identifiable category. Borderline grave; falls just under because no person is named.

**hype_charge** = -1 dry. Channel is not celebrating, not panicking, just observing.

**reel_intent** = `take`. The reel has a stance: "this is happening, and it's faster than people think."

**pacing_target** = 35-45s. 10-beat take.

**tonal_target_primary** = dark-humor. The system is the joke; designers as individuals are not. Channel posture: "we see what's happening."

**frame_zero_promise** = "Adobe replaced graphic designers in 12 seconds." (6 words.)

### Phase 1 rubric outputs

**R1 HOOK**: clause 4 fires partially (topic=drama/discourse), but actually closer to take. Manual selection: archetype #3 Contrarian + AA.11 shock/disgust. Hook claims a stance.

**R2 STRUCTURE**: clause 4 fires (hot-take, 35-45s). 10-beat take.

**R3 TONE**: dark-humor primary. Caption stack: DM Serif italic, low-contrast. EL tags `[dry][smirks]` + `[long pause]` before the punch.

**R4 EFFECTS**: dark-humor palette — halftone Ben-Day + zoom-out rug-pull + freeze-frame red-circle. **Forbidden by guardrail #2: confetti, money-rain, party-horn** because topic is industry-disruption-anxiety. **Forbidden by tone**: comic-pow, sad-trombone.

**R5 SFX**: sparse — ONE dry click on each scene cut. ONE sub-bass on the punchline beat. Room-tone bed at -28 LUFS continuous. **Forbidden: cha-ching** (guardrail #1 enforced because layoff-adjacent), party-horn, mario-coin. NO music bed.

**R6 IMAGE STYLE**: vintage-CRT or magazine-cutout collage. **Photoreal forbidden** — clashes with the irony register (guardrail #14).

**R7 MOTION VERB**: MID tier — pan, push-in, lean. Subtle camera moves on the demo clips; NO STRONG verbs (no slam/shatter — the topic is folding-of-an-industry, not explosive event).

| B3 (3s) | "In 12 seconds." | timer overlay, freeze-frame | sub-bass on "12" |

### Phase 3 critique pass (anticipated)

Likely passes most dims. Possible flags:
- Dim 6 (tone coherence): is the "anyway" beat really the punch? Could it land harder with no caption at all? Trade-off; let critique force a decision.
- Dim 8 (audience-fit): the dry-humor lands for AI-builder cohort; AI-curious might miss the irony. Litmus test: "would a smart non-engineer get this?" — yes, because the structure (setup → demo → undermined "anyway") is universal.

## Example 6 — Thin topic (escalation example)

**User says:** "make a reel about AI"

### Phase 0 intake

This is the example of the hard-gate failure case. Walk through:

**topic_class** = ??? — could be any of the 12. Cannot pick.

**emotional_thesis** — what feeling? "AI is interesting"? "AI is changing things"? Both fail the litmus (hand-waving, generic).

**share_trigger** — who would share this? "Anyone interested in AI" — that's not a share trigger, that's a topic.

**frame_zero_promise** — what does the cover deliver? "Today's AI thing"? Vague.

### Hard gate trips

The skill cannot draft. Phase 0 hard gate (in SKILL.md): if `emotional_thesis` AND `share_trigger` cannot both be written without hand-waving, escalate.

Response to user: 

"The topic is too thin to make a strong reel — I can't write a specific emotional thesis or share-trigger from 'AI' alone. To draft, I need either:

1. A specific source: a tweet URL, an article, a model launch, a benchmark result, a hot-take you want to make.
2. A specific stance: 'I want to argue that AI didn't kill design — it killed bad design' / 'I want to show what Veo 3 just shipped' / 'I want to make fun of the AI-influencer trend.'
3. A specific topic-class from this list: model-launch / feature-drop / benchmark-news / research-paper / acquisition / drama / culture-meme / tutorial / hot-take / death-or-layoff / safety-incident / regulation.

Which one? Or drop a tweet URL and I'll classify from the source."

This is the correct behavior — refuse to silently default. Defaults produce mid reels.

## Example 7 — Petdex (the actually-shipped reel, May 2026)

**Source material:** Three tweets — @OpenAIDevs (May 1, "Pets. Now in Codex.") + @RaillyHugo (May 2, "Built Petdex, a public gallery") + @tranmautritam (May 2, "Wake up babe, someone just built Petdex"). Story shape: OpenAI shipped /pet command in Codex on Friday; community shipped a public gallery + npm install in 14 hours; viral discovery within 18 more hours.

This is the most thoroughly-decoded session in the skill's history. The hook archetype, asset-generation pipeline, and tonal-palette interaction patterns documented in AA.18, AA.19, FF.16, and `prompts/meta-ai.md` were all validated against this reel.

### Phase 0 intake (after redo from scratch — see "lessons" below)

**topic_class** = `culture-meme` (with `feature-drop` as upstream seed). The whole story (OpenAI feature + community gallery + viral discovery + 192 pets) is community-velocity territory, not pure feature-drop.

**emotional_thesis** = "AGI panic looks ridiculous when the community ships a fintech koala." (11 words, ≤12 budget, specific feeling — relief + recognition + mild ridicule of the doomer frame.)

**audience_persona_lean** = `mixed-default`. AI-builder cohort gets the joke instantly; AI-curious cohort gets the reframe.

**hype_charge** = `-1 dry`. Channel posture is "let's calmly look at this and notice what it actually is."

**tonal_target_primary** = `ironic-deadpan` (single layer, no secondary close — Justine FF.6 register).

**share_trigger** = `inside-joke` primary + light `argument-bait` (the AGI-panic frame).

**frame_zero_promise** = "192 pets. 14 hours. this is AGI." (7 words; specific number + timestamp + ironic close-as-promise.)

### Phase 1 rubric outputs

- **R2 STRUCTURE**: `custom-8-beat-ironic-deadpan-thesis-noticing` — no documented template fits cleanly; closest analog is structures.md §6 6-beat micro (quiet-observation), extended with thesis-and-evidence shape.
- **R1 HOOK**: clause 0 fired (topic has clear company-protagonist Sam Altman) → archetype #1 + **AA.19 Multi-Instance Sora-Cameo Flex** — 50 stadium-Sams with pets, foreground close-up bewildered Sam.
- **R3 TONE**: ironic-deadpan single-layer (no secondary). Captions sentence-case Helvetica, room-tone bed only, NO music, NO cha-ching.
- **R4 EFFECTS**: 2 effects total — CSS dolly-in on hook + freeze-frame at timestamp punch.
- **R5 SFX**: 10 cues, room-tone-substituted by silence between cues.
- **R6 IMAGE STYLE**: photoreal-understated. Source assets only for body beats (Petdex screenshots + pet PNGs harvested from gallery). Hook still generated via Gemini Nano Banana Pro with `refs[]` pointing at Sam's real X profile pic.
- **R7 MOTION VERB**: SUBTLE tier. Most beats use harvested source-tweet videos (Playwright scroll-recordings of petdex.crafter.run + the OpenAI launch tweet's `/pet` demo). Hook animation via Meta AI image-to-video.

### Hook production pipeline (the multi-Sam stadium shot)

Validated path that *actually shipped* — recorded for replay on future celebrity-cameo reels:

1. **Get a real reference photo of the celebrity.** `curl "https://unavatar.io/twitter/sama" -o assets/research/petdex/sam-altman-real.jpg` — current Twitter profile pic, 32KB, 400×400, photoreal. (Wikipedia commons direct curl returned HTML error pages — unreliable.)

2. **Generate 5 still variants in parallel via Gemini Nano Banana Pro with `refs[]`**, each ~$0.13:
   - **v4 clean** — 4-clones-in-row (Varun-canonical baseline)
   - **v5 pile** — 1 clone overwhelmed by 20 pets, mildly bewildered face
   - **v6 pokeball** — clone mid-throw of Pokeball, pets bursting out in light
   - **v7 assembly** — 4 clones on conveyor belt, "PETS SHIPPED 1 OF 192" boxes (script-rhyme to B6 stat reveal)
   - **v8 stadium** — 50 clones in stadium seats, each holding a different unique pet, foreground bewildered close-up

   Total cost ~$0.65, total time ~2.5 minutes. Pick the funniest.

3. **Picked v8 stadium** — the scale-flex amplification of Varun's 4-Sundars pattern. Foreground bewildered Sam is the emotional anchor; 50 background Sams are the absurdity.

4. **Animate the picked still via Meta AI image-to-video** (Playwright + cookies):
   - New chat → upload `multisam-v8-stadium.png` → simple prompt `"animate this image"` → wait ~3 min → download response mp4
   - Output: 5s video with subtle motion (foreground Sam blinks/turns slightly, ambient depth-of-field breathing on the crowd)
   - Cost: $0 (cookie auth), wall time ~3-4 min

**Total hook asset budget**: ~$0.65 + ~6-8 minutes wall time. No Sora 2 needed; no Veo refusals; the Petdex hook shipped via Gemini-still + Meta-AI-animate.

### Lessons captured (the painful path that produced the skill upgrade)

The skill catalog AA.18 + AA.19 + FF.16 + `prompts/meta-ai.md` exists because of three corrections during this session:

## §7. How to use these examples

When drafting a reel:
1. Read the example whose `topic_class` matches your current topic.
2. Note the *reasoning trail* — how `emotional_thesis` was derived, why `tonal_target` was picked, why specific archetype.
3. Apply the same shape of reasoning to your reel — don't copy the outputs, copy the reasoning method.
4. If your reel's reasoning trail diverges meaningfully from the closest example, log the divergence in `editorial-brief.md` under `## Reasoning notes`.

Over time, the channel's `editorial-brief.md` archive becomes the *real* worked-examples library, calibrated to actual taste decisions made on real reels. This file is the seed; the channel is the tree.
